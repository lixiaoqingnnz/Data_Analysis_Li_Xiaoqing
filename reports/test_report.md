# BMW Used-Car Analytics Report

<div class="section-title">1 Trends by year</div>
<div class="subsection-title">1.1 Annual sales trends</div>
<img src="report_figures/sales_performance_year.png" class="report-image"/>
BMW's global sales volume and value exhibited steady long-term growth, with a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 1.8% for volume and 1.7% for value over the analyzed period. Peak performance was achieved in 2022, the highest year for both metrics, driven by robust demand for electric and premium models amid favorable market conditions and strategic pricing. This pinnacle year recorded the strongest growth rates, at 7.7% for volume and 7.2% for value, outperforming prior years. Post-2022 trends show moderation, with growth tapering toward the overall CAGR, signaling market normalization influenced by economic headwinds and heightened competition in the luxury segment.

<div class="subsection-title">1.2 Fuel type trend by year</div>
<img src="report_figures/fuel_type_grouped_bar_trend.png" class="report-image"/>
In the used-car market from 2020 to 2024, fuel type sales shares reveal a steady shift toward new energy vehicles, with Hybrid emerging as the dominant category in 2024 at 26.5% market share, up from 24.7% in 2020. Hybrid sales grew by 614,477 units over this period, reflecting consistent gains except for a dip to 24.7% in 2023. Electric vehicles followed a similar trajectory, peaking at 26.5% share and 4.31 million units in 2023 before a slight decline to 24.5% and 4.29 million units in 2024, with overall growth of 328,129 units from 2020. In contrast, traditional fuels showed stability with minor fluctuations: Diesel hovered around 24-25% share, peaking at 4.47 million units in 2022 but dipping to a low of 23.6% in 2023; Petrol remained steady near 24-25%, with no clear peak. This transition underscores rising demand for hybrids amid potential regulatory incentives and consumer preferences for efficiency, positioning new energy types at over 50% combined share by 2024.

<div class="subsection-title">1.3 Tranmission type (manual/automatic) trend by year</div>
<img src="report_figures/transmission_trend_grouped_bar_line.png" class="report-image"/>
In the used-car market from 2020 to 2024, transmission preferences shifted from automatic dominance to manual leadership. Automatic sales led in 2020 with 8,513,157 units versus 7,797,686 manuals, but manuals overtook in 2021 at 8,899,441 units compared to 7,985,225 automatics. Manual sales peaked in 2022 at 9,265,124 units—the highest across all categories and years—while automatics reached 8,655,822. Both categories dipped in 2023, with manuals at 8,212,274 and automatics at 8,056,380, likely reflecting broader market slowdowns. By 2024, manuals rebounded to 8,892,441 units, maintaining a lead over automatics at 8,635,413. This sustained manual preference post-2021 may stem from lower purchase prices, appeal in performance-oriented models like BMW's sportier variants, and enthusiast demand in the used segment, despite automatics' convenience.

<div class="section-title">2 Market segmentation by region</div>
<div class="subsection-title">2.1 Regional annual sales distribution</div>
<img src="report_figures/regional_sales_radar_chart.png" class="report-image"/>
Europe and the Middle East stand out as the leading regions by total annual sales, demonstrating robust and consistent performance across multiple years as depicted in the radar chart. These markets highlight BMW's strength in high-demand luxury segments, likely driven by favorable pricing strategies and popular SUV and electric model uptake. South America, conversely, remains the lowest performer, marking it as a clear outlier with noticeable volatility that warrants intensified market penetration efforts, such as localized model adaptations and promotional pricing. This distribution underscores the need to leverage European and Middle Eastern momentum while addressing South American underperformance to balance global sales growth.

<div class="subsection-title">2.2 Total sales volume by region</div>
<img src="report_figures/regional_sales_3d_map.png" class="report-image"/>
Europe leads in total sales volume with 14,565,989 units, significantly outpacing other regions and underscoring its dominance in the global BMW market. This standout performance is visualized prominently on the 3D extruded world map, where Europe's column rises tallest and darkest, reflecting its scale in million-unit sales. In contrast, South America records the lowest volume at 13,643,807 units, highlighting notable regional disparities despite the relatively close figures, which suggest untapped potential in emerging markets. These trends point to mature demand drivers in Europe, such as strong brand loyalty and premium model preferences, while South America's lower sales may stem from economic pressures or pricing sensitivities warranting targeted strategies to bridge the gap.

<div class="subsection-title">2.3 Price distribution by region</div>
<img src="report_figures/price_boxplot_by_region.png" class="report-image"/>
Car prices exhibit notable variation across regions, as depicted in the violin plot highlighting medians and distributional spreads. Asia leads with the highest median price of $77,497 USD, reflecting potentially stronger demand for premium BMW models among affluent consumers in that market. South America records the lowest median at $74,333 USD, suggesting more price-sensitive preferences or a focus on entry-level variants. The remaining regions cluster closely together: Africa at $75,598 USD, Europe at $75,458.50 USD, Middle East at $75,003 USD, and North America at $74,444 USD. This pattern indicates relatively tight price distributions globally, with Asia's elevated median pointing to opportunities for higher-margin sales, while South America's lower positioning underscores the need for competitive pricing strategies in emerging markets.

<div class="subsection-title">2.4 Fuel type distribution by region</div>
<img src="report_figures/fuel_type_by_region_facets.png" class="report-image"/>
Fuel type sales distribution across regions reveals tight competition among Diesel, Petrol, Electric, and Hybrid, with volumes consistently ranging from 3.3 million to 3.8 million units per category. Petrol leads in Africa (3,597,542 units) and the Middle East (3,696,511 units), reflecting strong demand for conventional internal combustion engines in these markets, likely driven by established infrastructure and cost sensitivities. Asia and North America favor Hybrid (3,697,983 and 3,752,979 units, respectively), indicating accelerating adoption of new energy vehicles amid urbanization and regulatory incentives for efficiency. Europe stands out with Electric dominating at 3,787,307 units, the highest volume overall, underscoring mature EV policies and charging networks. South America uniquely prioritizes Diesel (3,449,637 units), possibly tied to commercial vehicle preferences and biofuel compatibility. Notable regional differences include Europe's clear pivot to pure Electric, contrasting with Hybrid's edge in high-growth Asia and North America, while traditional fuels persist in Africa, Middle East, and South America. Outliers are minimal, but Europe's Electric sales exceed its nearest regional rival (Middle East Electric at 3,644,552) by over 140,000 units, signaling a potential blueprint for global electrification strategies. Overall, new energy types (Electric and Hybrid) top or closely trail leaders in five of six regions, pointing to a gradual shift away from pure Diesel and Petrol dominance.

<div class="section-title">3 Product and consumer preferences</div>
<div class="subsection-title">3.1 Model performance ranking</div>
<img src="report_figures/top_bottom_models_barh.png" class="report-image"/>
The model performance ranking reveals a highly competitive landscape, with total sales volumes across all years and regions clustering tightly between 7.4 million and 8.2 million units. Leading the pack is the 7 Series with 8,177,442 units sold, closely followed by the X6 at 8,099,240 units and the M5 at 7,807,649 units, underscoring strong demand for luxury sedans and premium performance SUVs. In contrast, the M3, i3, and X5 trail at the bottom with 7,571,963, 7,544,460, and 7,449,141 units respectively, highlighting potential vulnerabilities in compact performance models, electric vehicles like the i3, and mid-size SUVs amid shifting consumer preferences toward higher-end luxury offerings. This narrow spread suggests opportunities for targeted enhancements in underperforming segments, such as expanding electric model appeal or refining SUV lineups to close the gap with top performers.

<div class="subsection-title">3.2 Mileage impact on sales</div>
<img src="report_figures/sales_by_mileage.png" class="report-image"/>
Analysis of mileage impact on used-car sales shows that high mileage vehicles (>120,000 km) generate the highest volume at 34,576,718 units, narrowly surpassing mid mileage (40,000-120,000 km) with 33,728,749 units. Low mileage cars (<40,000 km) lag considerably at 16,607,496 units, accounting for less than 20% of total sales. Together, mid and high mileage groups dominate the market at over 80% of sales, suggesting buyers favor more affordable options despite higher wear, likely driven by price sensitivity rather than condition preferences. This trend highlights an opportunity to target budget segments with reliable high-mileage models.

<div class="subsection-title">3.3 Color preference analysis</div>
<img src="report_figures/color_sales_bar.png" class="report-image"/>
In the used-car market, Black emerges as the top preferred color, accounting for 14,450,883 units in total sales, surpassing all others by a margin of approximately 760,000 units over the least popular Grey at 13,690,884 units. Red and White follow closely with 14,298,208 and 14,273,392 units respectively, while Blue and Silver trail at 14,163,128 and 14,036,468 units. This narrow range across all six colors—spanning less than 6% from highest to lowest—indicates relatively even consumer preferences, with no dominant outlier. Black's lead may reflect its perceived premium appeal and versatility in resale value, though the tight clustering suggests minimal differentiation driven by color alone in purchasing decisions.

<div class="subsection-title">3.4 Engine-size market preference</div>
<img src="report_figures/engine_size_sales_bar.png" class="report-image"/>
In the used-car market, engine-size preferences strongly favor mid-to-large displacement categories, which collectively dominate sales volumes. The 3.1–4.0L high-displacement segment leads with 24,674,355 units sold, slightly ahead of the 2.1–3.0L mid-size or premium engines at 24,546,679 units and the >4.0L performance or luxury engines at 22,608,551 units. These three larger categories account for approximately 85% of total sales, underscoring a clear market tilt toward vehicles offering greater power, refinement, and premium features, likely driven by buyer priorities for performance and brand prestige over fuel efficiency in established models. In contrast, smaller engines lag significantly: the <=1.5L category records just 1,168,111 sales (under 2% of the market), while 1.6–2.0L compact mainstream engines manage 11,915,267 units (around 14%), highlighting limited appeal for entry-level options amid rising demand for upscale driving experiences. This distribution suggests opportunities for BMW to emphasize its high-displacement lineup in marketing and inventory strategies.
